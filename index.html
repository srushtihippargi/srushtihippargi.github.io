My research interests lie at the intersection of Computer Vision, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning for Robotics, with a particular focus on Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM), Robot Navigation, and Probabilistic Modeling in Robotics. I have worked extensively on projects involving scene understanding, semantic mapping, 3D reconstruction, and uncertainty modeling for robotic perception. Additionally, I have knowledge of state estimation techniques, multi-modal sensor fusion, and optimization methods for autonomous systems.

Currently, I am exploring Lie groups and Equivariant Deep Learning techniques to enhance perception and control frameworks in robotics. I am actively involved in research at the Computational Autonomy and Robotics Laboratory (CURLY Lab) under the guidance of Professor Maani Ghaffari, where my work focuses on Scene Graph-Based Vision-Language Navigation and Temporally Fused Scene Completion. Through these projects, I have gained hands-on experience in designing and implementing deep learning models for robotic navigation and 3D scene understanding.
